Project Iroduction
  • Our group’s design project is an external mounted wheelchair stair climbing device. This device can be installed on traditional wheelchairs, aiming to help elderly people overcome the problem of stairs in areas without wheelchair access and residential buildings without elevators.
  • We recognize that stairs are a huge challenge for people with limited mobility. And our equipment will provide them with a convenient solution. This device adopts advanced technology and can help wheelchair users safely and stably climb and descend stairs.
  • Our device has the following characteristics: firstly, it is externally mounted and can be compatible with various types of traditional wheelchairs, without the need to replace the entire wheelchair. Secondly, our device adopts an intelligent control system that can perform precise operations based on user actions and intentions. In addition, it is equipped with an efficient battery system to ensure long-term use and stability.
  • Our goal is to provide a reliable, safe, and easy-to-use solution that allows people with limited mobility to move and participate in social activities more freely. Through our design, we hope to improve their quality of life and enable them to independently cope with accessibility challenges. Our team will actively develop and test this device, and collaborate with relevant institutions and communities to ensure that our design meets user needs and truly solves their problems with stairs. We believe that through our efforts and innovation, we can bring positive impacts to people with limited mobility and bring more tolerance and care to society.

Our final model is shown in the following figure.
  • During the actual testing process, we found that the middle part of our board still had a tendency to collide with the stairs. This requires us to change its size or choose to change the size of the wheels. Moreover, more importantly, we realize that the biggest problem of using a Samsung wheel to go upstairs is starting to surface. We initially believed that the problem with only one set of intermediate gears was that running on flat ground and running on stairs would result in a difference in forward and reverse rotation of the central gear, so we added a set of gears. However, in real practice, it has been found that no matter how many sets of gears are added, forward rotation and reverse rotation are inevitable, because forward rotation and reverse rotation are determined by the nature of the gears themselves. This mode of reverse rotation needs to be consistent with the rotation direction of the gears that are coaxial with the wheel and mesh with it, which is impossible. This is the biggest problem we have encountered and urgently needs to be solved.


Physical Progress 1 of the Semester (02/29/2024 - 03/27/2024)
  • Due to many design issues in the first half of our senior year, and the continuous improvement process, the physical situation has become increasingly complex. Therefore, we have decided to adopt a new upstairs structure and method. The basic wheelchair structure is shown in the following figure and has been purchased. The design principle of the upstairs device is also shown in the figure.
  • The physical object of the rocker arm stepper structure of the core components upstairs is shown in the figure. The matching small rubber wheels are as shown in the picture.
  • The structure of stepping up the stairs is based on the flipping of the rocker arm and the contact with the steps, so the size (radius) of the rocker arm must be larger than the wheel (radius) of the wheelchair's rear wheel. We use different axis methods to achieve this process, because we only need to allow the movement of the rocker arm to exceed the radius of the rear wheel below the wheelchair. However, the size of the rear wheel of the wheelchair frame is too large, so we have purchased new wheels (smaller than the rocker arm size), as shown in the picture. The reason for using inflatable wheels is that it is inevitable that the rear wheels will collide with the steps during the process of stepping up the stairs, which we do not want. The use of inflatable wheels can appropriately reduce vibration.
  • The dimensions of the rocker arm, small rubber wheel, inflatable wheel, and the distance between the two axes are shown in the figure.
  • We then purchased the motor, battery, and customized a controller, as shown in the picture. The controller includes a switch that can switch between up and down stairs mode (rocker arm forward and reverse), a knob to adjust speed, and finally a simple display screen that displays the remaining voltage and power in conjunction with the battery. For the convenience of operation, we placed the button for controlling the movement of the rocker arm (whether it is powered on or not) on a handrail, and designed it in a form that can adjust the height (length). We realized that the appropriate height of the wheelchair is often not suitable when running on flat ground, and longer handrails are needed during the process of going up and down, as shown in the figure.
  • Then we combined the parts and welded them all onto a large steel plate, as shown in the picture. Then, we removed the excess part of the wheelchair frame and had the master cut off the parts that hindered the welding, as shown in the picture. Finally, weld these two parts together, and the finished product is shown in the picture.
